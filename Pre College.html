ERAN HAKKI
Economics and Law ECON P-16535 (35473)
July 25 - August 5, 2022
This course introduces students to the economic analysis of law, a tool increasingly used in evaluating the efficiency of legal rules and in
understanding the interactions between these rules and human behavior. We cover the primary areas of common law: property, contract, tort, and
criminal law, applying an economic perspective to the problems within each. By the end of the course, students are able to analyze a broad range
of legal topics and to bring economic concepts to bear on issues of public policy and discussions of the law.
Eran is a well-rounded student. He performed as well on the mathematical problems of the course as on the argumentative and analytical. Never
reluctant to tackle difficult questions, he always ventured well-thought-out responses that challenged his peers. His work on the problem sets and
the exam was excellent, but most impressive was his performance during the mock trial at the end of the course. Not only was Eran's approach
intellectually cogent, but he also delivered his argument in a rhetorically persuasive way and knew how to collaborate with others. It goes without
saying that Eran will excel in any academic environment and whatever discipline he engages in, be it law, economics, or any other.
Alley Edlebi
Doctoral Candidate in Political Economy and Government, Harvard University
